Body Mass Index
Obesity and physical inactivity are two big risk factors when it comes to diabetes. The Body Mass Index is a relatively simple (and cost effective) method of screening for both factors. It is worth noting that the BMI is not perfect as it does not consider some important variables such as muscle mass or ethnicity.

Blood Glucose
Our model uses your blood glucose (in mg/dL) measured two hours after an oral glucose tolerance test. This test helps identify any abnormalities in the way that your body processes the glucose that you obtain from your daily meals.

Blood Pressure
Your diastolic blood pressure (the bottom number) is the pressure present in the arteries when your heart rests in between beats. Diabetes can damage your arteries, causing them to harden in a condition known as atherosclerosis. This has the potential to cause high blood pressure which can lead to more serious conditions such as heart attack and kidney failure if left untreated.
